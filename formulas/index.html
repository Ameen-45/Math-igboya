Algebra Formulas

Quadratic Formula

Algebra • Equations

x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a

Used to find the roots of a quadratic equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0

  • a, b, c Coefficients of the quadratic equation
  • x Roots of the equation
Basic

Binomial Theorem

Algebra • Expansion

(a + b)ⁿ = Σ [n! / (k!(n-k)!)] · aⁿ⁻ᵏ · bᵏ

Expresses the expansion of a binomial raised to any positive integer power

  • a, b Terms of the binomial
  • n Exponent (positive integer)
  • k Index of summation (0 to n)
Intermediate

Arithmetic Sequence

Algebra • Sequences

aₙ = a₁ + (n - 1)d

Finds the nth term of an arithmetic sequence where each term increases by a constant difference

  • aₙ nth term of the sequence
  • a₁ First term of the sequence
  • d Common difference
Basic

Geometry Formulas

Pythagorean Theorem

Geometry • Triangles

a² + b² = c²

Relates the sides of a right triangle, where c is the hypotenuse

  • a, b Legs of the right triangle
  • c Hypotenuse of the triangle
Basic

Area of a Circle

Geometry • Circles

A = πr²

Calculates the area enclosed by a circle

  • A Area of the circle
  • r Radius of the circle
  • π Pi, approximately 3.14159
Basic

Volume of a Sphere

Geometry • 3D Shapes

V = (4/3)πr³

Calculates the volume of a sphere

  • V Volume of the sphere
  • r Radius of the sphere
  • π Pi, approximately 3.14159

Calculus Formulas

Power Rule

Calculus • Derivatives

d/dx [xⁿ] = nxⁿ⁻¹

Finds the derivative of a power function

  • x Variable
  • n Exponent (real number)
Basic

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Calculus • Integration

∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx = F(b) - F(a)

Relates differentiation and integration, where F is the antiderivative of f

  • f(x) Function to integrate
  • F(x) Antiderivative of f(x)
  • a, b Limits of integration
Advanced

Chain Rule

Calculus • Derivatives

d/dx [f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) · g'(x)

Finds the derivative of a composite function

  • f, g Differentiable functions
  • f', g' Derivatives of f and g
Intermediate

Trigonometry • Identities

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Fundamental trigonometric identity relating sine and cosine

Law of Sines

Trigonometry • Triangles

a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC

Relates the sides and angles of any triangle

  • a, b, c Sides of the triangle
  • A, B, C Angles opposite to sides a, b, c
